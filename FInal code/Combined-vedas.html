Introduction

Vedas

Vedic knowledge is the knowledge that is contained in the ancient scriptures of the Indian tradition, primarily the Vedas. It is a profound body of spiritual wisdom that guides individuals towards self-realization, understanding the cosmos, and attaining peace and liberation (moksha).

There are 4 types of Vedas

Rigveda

The Rigveda or Rig Veda (Sanskrit: ऋग्वेद, IAST: ṛgveda, from ऋच्, "praise" and वेद, "knowledge") is an ancient Indian collection of Vedic Sanskrit hymns (sūktas). It is one of the four sacred canonical Hindu texts (śruti) known as the Vedas. Only one Shakha of the many survives today, namely the Śakalya Shakha.

Yajurveda

The Yajurveda (Sanskrit: यजुर्वेद, IAST: yajurveda, from यजुस्, "worship", and वेद, "knowledge") is the Veda primarily of prose mantras for worship rituals. An ancient Vedic Sanskrit text, it is a compilation of ritual-offering formulas that were said by a priest while an individual performed ritual actions such as those before the yajna fire.

Samaveda

The Samaveda (Sanskrit: सामवेद, IAST: Sāmaveda, from सामन्, "song" and वेद, "knowledge"), is the Veda of melodies and chants. It is an ancient Vedic Sanskrit text, and one of the sacred scriptures in Hinduism. One of the four Vedas, it is a liturgical text which consists of 1,875 verses.

Atharvaveda

The Atharvaveda or Atharva Veda (Sanskrit: अथर्ववेद, IAST: Atharvaveda, from अथर्वन्, "priest" and वेद, "knowledge") is the "knowledge storehouse of atharvāṇas, the procedures for everyday life". The text is the fourth Veda and is a late addition to the Vedic scriptures of Hinduism.

Internal Blocks of the Vedas

Samhita

Constitutes the main portion of each Veda. It consists of a certain number of mantras presented in metrical form.

Brahmanas

A large portion of the Vedic repository. It is a collection of knowledge related to rituals and rites. It describes different types of yajnas in detail and serves as an instruction manual for altar construction.

Aranyakas

Takes a philosophical approach while discussing yajna, focusing on the symbolism and philosophical aspects of rituals.

Upanishads

Contains the loftiest thoughts of the Vedic seers. The word 'Upanishad' means sitting near a guru to receive wisdom through a teaching-learning process. Originally, there were 1180 Upanishads.

External Blocks of the Vedas (Vedangas)

Siksha

The science of pronunciation. Siksha-sastra is a systematic approach to phonetics, focusing on correct pronunciation and articulation of words.

Vyakarana

The study of Sanskrit grammar and linguistic analysis. It involves the conciseness of Panini's Astadhyayi, analyzing the structure of the language.

Nirukta

Nirukta means etymology. It explains the reason behind the use of a particular word, focusing on the meaning of usage.

Kalpa

The origin of Kalpa was for organizing the extensive rituals described in the Vedas. Kalpas are texts that explain rituals systematically and may omit irrelevant aspects.

Chandas

The Samhita portion of the Vedas is concerned with poetic composition. Chandas preserves the Vedas and literature in prosody, without scope for adding or removing syllables.

Jyothishya

The Vedanga Jyotisha describes astronomical events, including the winter solstice, which has been used to date the Vedic texts. It is written in two recensions: Rigveda and Yajurveda.
