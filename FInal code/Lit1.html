Vedic Literature

Classifications of Vedic Literature

Vedic literature is classified into two categories: Shruti and Smriti. Let’s discuss each in detail:

  • Shruti: Sacred texts comprising Vedas, Brahmanas, Aranyakas, and Upanishads. Passed through oral traditions, these texts contain eternal knowledge.
  • Smriti: Texts based on memory and written by post-Vedic sages. Includes Itihasas, Puranas, Vedangas, and other classical Sanskrit literature.

Features of Vedic Literature

  • Divided into two timelines: Older Vedic and Later Vedic.
  • Fields formed include philosophy, algebra, ethics, astronomy, and more.
  • Emphasizes meditation, the cycle of birth and death, and values like mercy, self-control, and karma.
  • Prominent women include Ghosha, Sulabha, Maitreyi, Lopamudra, and Gargi.
  • Allied literature includes Vedangas, Darshanas, Sutras, and Upavedas.
  • Mentions environmental awareness and preservation for all living beings.

Vedic Shruti Literature

Brahmanas

Prose works explaining Vedic hymns, sacrifices, and rites. Examples include Aitareya and Kausitaki Brahmanas.

Aranyakas

Known as “forest books,” intended for hermits. Discuss symbolism, mysticism, and provide a bridge to Upanishads.

Upanishads

Philosophical texts also known as Vedanta, focusing on intellectual and spiritual learning. The motto Satyameva Jayate is derived from the Mundakopanishad.

Vedic Smriti Literature

  • Vedangas: Six limbs of the Vedas—Siksha (pronunciation), Kalpa (rituals), Vyakarana (grammar), Nirukta (etymology), Chhanda (meter), and Jyotisa (astrophysics).
  • Shatdarsana: Six schools of philosophy—Nyaya, Vaisheshika, Sankhya, Yoga, Purva Mimamsa, and Uttar Mimamsa.
  • Puranas: Ancient texts dealing with mythology, history, and moral teachings.
  • Itihasas: Epic tales like the Ramayana and Mahabharata.
  • Tantras: Literature focusing on worship through Agamas, Nigamas, and Samhitas.
  • Agamas: Instructional guides for worship, covering knowledge, rituals, and theology.
  • Upavedas: Applied knowledge texts, including Ayurveda (medicine), Dhanurveda (warfare), Gandharvaveda (music), and Silpaveda (art and architecture).
